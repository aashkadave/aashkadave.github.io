Mexican Food in the UK

Anecdotally speaking, there's not much Mexican food in the UK. Some would go so far as to say the situation is sad, others would say it's desperate. This set of charts attempts to shed light on this mystery, by focusing on a single question:

Is Mexican food in the UK a thing?

To find out if anecdotal experience aligns across borders, I asked several individuals what they thought about the present Mexican-food-in-UK-situation. Here's what they had to say:

“When I joyfully studied abroad in the UK in 2013, I expected uniform [i.e., mostly British] food. But I wasn’t emotionally prepared for the lack of Mexican and Tex-Mex cuisine. My overall experience was amazing. But queso access would have made the trip extraordinary.” -Anonymous, from Georgia
“In the UK [burritos] are basically luxury snack food for middle or upper class people working at Canary Wharf (basically British Wall Street)."-Anonymous, from the UK
“Well there are good Mexican-*inspired* restaurants..."-Anonymous, from the UK
"It just wasn't there. The best we found had a substitute for every ingredient — the queso tasted like it was made of Camembert, and the salsa smelled distinctly of Italian herbs. Wrong vibe."- Anonymous, from the US

So how can we go about figuring out whether Mexican food is a Thing Thing in the UK? Thanks to Jay Rayner, we have some options!

Of course, this begs a question: who is Jay Rayner?

Jay Rayner is a food critic and reviewer for The Guardian, and has been so for a very long time. His reviews are prolific (and often comical). Since he's been around for so long, we're using his food reviews as a benchmark.

Does Jay Rayner, Voice of British Food Reviews and Tastes, talk about Mexican food? If yes, how? And what does this mean for those of us who go to the UK and just want a good taco?
